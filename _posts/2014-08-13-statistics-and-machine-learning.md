Below are some introductory texts for probability, statistics, machine learning and statistical learning theory. They are sorted according to difficulty, so you can find a book that is suitable for where you are and what you need.

1. Introductory books by Sheldon Ross

   a. This covers combinatorics and stuff. For undergraduates. Might be too easy. [A First Course in Probability (9th Edition)](http://www.amazon.com/First-Course-Probability-9th-Edition/dp/032179477X/ref=dp_ob_title_bk) _Sheldon M. Ross_

   b. Covers more advanced topics. Lots of examples. [Introduction to Probability and Statistics for Engineers and Scientists (4th Edition)](http://www.amazon.com/Introduction-Probability-Statistics-Engineers-Scientists/dp/0123704839) _Sheldon M. Ross_

2. An alternative to (1a)+(1b). More systematic but it doesn’t cover advanced topics like (1b). This book was used by MIT for the following [course](http://www.pearsonhighered.com/educator/product/Probability-and-Statistics-4E/9780321500465.page). There are some simplified lecture notes on the course website. [Probability and Statistics, 4/E](http://www.pearsonhighered.com/educator/product/Probability-and-Statistics-4E/9780321500465.page) _Morris H. DeGroot and Mark J. Schervish_

3. Here are some introductory slides I made for a summer workshop at Notre Dame University in 2013 to undergraduate math students. The first is on [Probability, Statistics, Statistical learning](http://www1.i2r.a-star.edu.sg/~lins/files/cmnd1SLT.pdf). The second is on [Graphical Models](http://www1.i2r.a-star.edu.sg/~lins/files/cmnd3GM.pdf).

4. The author is my FAVORITE guy on statistical learning. Assumes knowledge of probability and statistics, see (1a), (1b) and (2). Jordan has some [old slides](http://www.cis.upenn.edu/~mkearns/papers/barbados/jordan-tut.pdf) for a tutorial he was teaching. [An Introduction to Graphical Models (not yet published)](https://www.sites.google.com/site/kittipat/good-machine-learning-resources/anintroductiontographicalmodels) _Michael Jordan_

5. Introductory books by Robert Tibshirani who is one of the fathers of LASSO (linear regression with sparsity).

   a. Standard introductory UNDERGRADUATE text for statistical learning. Assumes knowledge of probability and statistics, see (1a), (1b) and (2). [An Introduction to Statistical Learning with Applications in R](http://www-bcf.usc.edu/~gareth/ISL/) _Gareth James, Daniela Witten, Trevor Hastie and Robert Tibshirani_

   b.  Standard introductory GRADUATE text for statistical learning. Assumes knowledge of probability and statistics, see (1a), (1b) and (2). If you master this textbook, you can truly be an expert in machine learning. [The Elements of Statistical Learning: Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction (2nd Edition)](http://statweb.stanford.edu/~tibs/ElemStatLearn/) _Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani and Jerome Friedman_

6. An advanced online textbook on Statistics: [http://www.statlect.com/](http://www.statlect.com/). A good formal treatment of probability theory can be found [here](https://www.math.duke.edu/~rtd/PTE/PTE4_1.pdf).

7. All of Statistics - Larry Wasserman

8. Mathematical Statistics - Jun Shao
