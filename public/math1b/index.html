Notes

Big Ideas

My philosophy for math is that it is better to understand the big important concepts and ideas, than to memorize a bunch of formula. I wrote this set of notes in hope of helping students do that, so it is not meant to be a complete summary of the course material.

I also included some comments from my looking at the homework problems and quizzes, such as useful techniques and common mistakes. It will be updated from time to time, as the class progresses. Feel free to email me if you would like certain concepts broken down and explained.

Online Programmable Calculator

Here's a useful free online programmable calculator powered by Octave, good for tedious computations such as approximating integrals using Simpson's rule.

http://www.online-utility.org/math/math_calculator.jsp

To compute, say Q5 of Section 7.7, you can use the code

function y = f (x)
t = x*pi/8;
y = t^2*sin(t);
endfunction
(pi/8)/3*(f(0)+4*f(1)+2*f(2)+4*f(3)+2*f(4)+4*f(5)+2*f(6)+4*f(7)+f(8))
