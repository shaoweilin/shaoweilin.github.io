Boltzmann machines and hierarchical models

  • 02 August 2014

TheÂ restricted Boltzmann machine (RBM) is a key statistical model used in deep learning. They are special form of Boltzmann machines where the underlying graph is a bipartite graph. Personally, I am more interested in Boltzmann machines because they represent a class of discrete energy models where the energy is quadratic. The dynamics of the model bears a lot of resemblance to those of Hopfield networks and Ising models. As an aside, normal distributions are continuous energy models where the energy is quadratic and positive definite.
